Proposed Changes to Standing Orders

  1. MOTION: Addition of ‘Don’t use the library as a long term storage place’ to the Library Rules
    • 12 in favour; 0 against; 0 abstaining; 0 not voting; 2 can’t voting
  2. MOTION: Addition of ‘No staying in the library past 11pm without a committee member present or permission from the librarian’ to the Library Rules
    • 12 in favour; 0 against; 0 abstaining; 0 not voting; 2 can’t voting
  3. Addition of Key List Policy
    • Last year people weren’t aware of key list responsibility. We’ve got policy written now; people will sign the form when added to the key list
      • How does signing help? Symbolic thing, to give trustworthy people an impression of responsibility. If people break the rules -> removal from the key list and ban from the library (last resort). Banning seems awkward because it denies access to the club they paid for. We can’t stop people from borrowing books, but we can ban them from coming into the social space
      • What things are considered as breaking the rules? Forgetting to lock up; leaving large numbers of personal things; sleeping; not cleaning up; people not instructed how to take the book out and books went missing
      • Where do we keep the forms? In France drawer
    • Recommendation: keep copy of the key list on the door
    • MOTION: 12 in favour; 0 against; 0 abstaining; 0 not voting; 2 can’t voting
  4. Addition of Safer Space policy
    • The union has one policy and it is outdated so Ed tried to write one for Sci Fi. It doesn’t add much to what we do in the library but it makes it more clear if something bad happens (precaution). Still waiting for DPW approval of our safer space policy draft. Safer space policy – thoroughly explained ‘Don’t take the piss’ rule. Quotes list falls under banter; it is usually without context so we could filter things to be put on the website. How about trial period and see how it goes for few months before we get feedback from DPW. We can see if any issues arise
    • What will happen in 5 years' time when a new committee could misinterpret the policy written by Ed? We can put an expiry date on the policy – e.g. it needs to be reapproved by current committee in the next 3 years.
    • Does the union care about safer space policy? Ed says that we want something that comes under Union, not a separate thing because we don’t have the authority. The union policy is 4 years old and is difficult to find. Ed wants to make people aware of it because it will be harder to abuse it
    • People aren’t thrown out every day; it’s only exceptional circumstances. Documentation should be last resort. In policy there is a path of action, e.g. talk to neutral committee member
    • Can’t we have aims for library social space, like 9 rules on the door to remind people? People usually don’t know about standing orders when they join society. Also we aren’t trained, whereas Union has people who are trained; that’s why our policy would refer to Union policy
    • Writing rules down make it more like walking on egg shells. Older members will be affected because new committee may be more strict with interpretation of the rules
    • Chris: Back in Sci Fi there was a man named Adam; he lacked empathy. He started hitting me with a Millwall hammer (rolled newspaper) – it hurt; I had bruises the next day. We talked to him and Adam turned into a nice person. Situations like that come up, but you can deal with it rather than turn someone to a piece of paper. I’m sure, if a safer space policy was written, someone would kick me out of here. Another valid point – documents change perspective when situations like that happen (‘he’s a dick’ -> ‘I’ve been assaulted’)
    • For the policy – more clarifications on what levels of escalations are appropriate. Union never enforced their policy; they mention web form that doesn’t exist. We can remove mentions of Union, which would be a wrong thing to do. Higher people would be offended that we don’t mention it
