    • The first Friday of Freshers’ Week clashes with Freshers’ Ball, so will be held on Thursday 5th October instead, with a 6pm start.

    • Will watch the films through beforehand to design the bingo.

    • Scheduled for Saturday 7th October.

    • Will also consider somewhere to get food during the day.
    • If we visit any second hand bookstores, may also be worth enquiring about whether they’d be willing to come to Picocon, since one of the previous book vendors can no longer make it.
  • Literary Lucky Dip:
    • Provisionally scheduled for Wednesday 11th October, from 12 to 2pm and then 6 to 10pm, to open up the window of opportunity to people who do other activities like sports and gaming.
    • We have some quite nice books with duplicate copies, and could include some of these.
    • Will also hand out a few random older books at Freshers’ Fair (these will not be wrapped up so people can choose them).
  • Mental Health Day:
    • Tuesday 10th October
    • Plan for event:
      • Will have a stall in Beit, directing people towards the library, where we will be showing movies. Will have a safer spaces policy for the event, showing either relaxing escapist films or something more relevant to mental health.
      • Motion proposed to concentrate primarily on media that deal with mental health.
      • MOTION: 1 for escapism, 7 for mental health, 1 abstaining.
      • Decided to initially focus on specific things that deal with mental health, provided they’re not overly dark, and then potentially move onto more relaxing things afterwards as the day goes on.
      • ACTION: All to email Amelia with suggestions for films.
      • ACTION: Amelia to make a list of suggestions for discussion.
      • ACTION: Kai to make a rota for people to be in the library and on the stall in Beit.
    • Safer spaces policy:
      • Ed’s draft for a policy currently is effective at specifying the policies enough to avoid restricting people’s fun, while still making the room a more comfortable place.
      • However, currently directs people towards a union web-form that is inactive.
      • ACTION: Henry to speak to DPW to check the union’s policy and ensure having our own policy is okay. Also to check whether the union web-form will become active, and whether there are any steps we could take to deal with issues that aren’t serious enough to be raised to the union.
      • Currently we only have the ability to remove people from the library, but could potentially also use temporary bans as a milder alternative to a permanent ban.
      • Could introduce a form to sign if you are placed on the keylist, for people to agree that they are responsible and will follow the society’s rules, and that we could remove them from the list if necessary.
      • The mental health event will be a trial run for a more official safer spaces policy that might be active in the library at other times.
      • A note will be put at the top of the policy to clarify that it is currently designed for the mental health event, and to open it up to thoughts from other members.
      • The policy would ultimately be enforced by the Librarian and Chair, but in practice by people on the keylist who are acting as Duty Librarians (in the same way that they have some responsibilities to maintain the library itself).
      • It could in theory eventually be introduced officially as an appendix to the standing orders, but for now could be discussed at the EGM and potentially included into the standing orders at that point, depending on feedback and a vote if it reaches that point.
